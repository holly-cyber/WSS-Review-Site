The first thing I noticed about the Veloforte Nova Protein Recovery Shake was the **gorgeous flavour and silky-smooth texture**. I tried the **banana and cocoa blend with maca**, and it genuinely surprised me. Too many recovery shakes can taste chalky or artificial, but this one was the opposite—rich, chocolatey, and slightly fruity without being overpowering.

I mixed mine with semi-skimmed milk, and it turned into a creamy shake. There were no lumps, even without using a shaker ball, which is a big plus. I also threw a few ice cubes into my thermos mug, and after my pool session, it was still ice cold and delicious. ## Nutrition Profile

Veloforte has a reputation for creating sports nutrition that's as natural as it is effective, and the Nova Recovery Protein Shake fits the bill. When mixed with cow's milk, one serving packs in 13g of protein, 40g of carbs & re-vitalising Maca**,** perfect for muscle repair and recovery. The protein blend is designed to be absorbed quickly, which is why Veloforte recommends consuming it within 30 minutes of finishing your workout.

Alongside protein, you'll also get carbohydrates to help replenish glycogen stores, as well as maca, which is often used for its potential to support energy balance and endurance. The ingredients feel clean and purposeful, which is refreshing in a market where many recovery shakes are loaded with fillers or unnecessary additives. However, it does have a little less protein than some others on the market. would ideally like to get a little more protein in each shake to aid my overall daily protein intake.

> Dr. Stacy Sims recommends that active women consume 1.7 to 2.4 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight daily, with slightly higher amounts (2.2 to 2.4 g/kg/day) for women in perimenopause or menopause.

Normally, I'd be tempted by something sweet and not particularly recovery-friendly, but this shake kept me feeling satisfied and fuelled until my next meal. That in itself is a huge win, as poor post-workout choices often derail progress.

Psychologically, too, it was a boost. Knowing I had a protein recovery shake that actually tasted like a treat gave me something to look forward to and helped me stay consistent with my recovery routine. Over time, that consistency will pay off in training gains.
