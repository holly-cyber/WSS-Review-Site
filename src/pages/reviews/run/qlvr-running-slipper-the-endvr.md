## WSS™ Assessment

### Inclusive Fit & Proportional Sizing — 5/5 ★★★★★

This is where QLVR earns its strongest marks, and I say that having worn plenty of shoes that claim women-specific fit while quietly running off a scaled male last. The ENDVR is built on a true female-specific footbed — narrower heel, wider forefoot, higher arch profile — and I felt that immediately. There's no uncomfortable pinching at the heel or squeezing at the toe box that I'd normally accept as inevitable.

The sizing does run slightly small, and QLVR is upfront about this: most customers go up half a size, and wider-footed wearers go up a full size. At £123.75, I'd prefer a shoe that nails true-to-size fit out of the box, and that caveat is worth flagging. However, the brand publishes a foot-measurement sizing chart on qlvr.com rather than relying on vague S/M/L labelling, which mitigates the guesswork considerably. The size range spans UK 3.5–9, which covers a solid spread, though I'd like to see it extended toward a UK 10+ in future iterations. Critically, there is no XL labelling of mid-range sizes here, and the proportional engineering reflects genuine female foot geometry rather than marketing language. That warrants a 5.

---

### Functional Performance in Use — 4/5 ★★★★☆

I've worn these through steady-state runs, interval sessions, and strength circuits, and the performance is genuinely impressive for a crossover shoe. The full-length bio-based Pebax® propulsion plate delivers a springy, energy-returning feel that makes faster efforts feel efficient rather than laboured. The rocker profile and deep flex grooves guide your foot through its natural stride, and the midfoot "piston" structure adds a responsiveness that I didn't expect from something called a "slipper."

The WingFit™ system — a patented laceless mechanism made from bio-based Pebax® polymer — expands as your foot enters and contracts to deliver a 360° locked-in hold. In practice, it works. Nothing slipped during lateral movements or faster intervals, and I haven't had to re-tie a single lace (obviously), which alone improves my run rhythm more than I'd like to admit.

Where I'd pull back one star: the absence of published stack height, heel-to-toe drop measurement, or outsole traction data makes it genuinely difficult to recommend this shoe for all surfaces or gait types with full confidence. I experienced no chafing or hot spots past the 45-minute mark, but runners who pronate significantly or require structured stability features should seek independent gait assessment before committing at this price. The performance I experienced was strong; I just can't fully verify the technical specifications behind it.

---

### Adequate Support Standards — 5/5 ★★★★★

The ENDVR is a training and running shoe, not a sports bra, so traditional impact-level and cup-range criteria don't apply directly here. Assessed through the lens of structural foot support — the domain relevant to this category — I found the shoe to provide consistent, confidence-inspiring hold throughout varied sessions. The WingFit™ system's 360° compression prevented heel lift and lateral foot movement during cross-training drills. The Pebax® propulsion plate offers meaningful energy return without the instability that some highly cushioned shoes introduce. I experienced no joint discomfort after back-to-back training days, which is a meaningful signal. No support failures to report.

---

### Genuine Female Design — 5/5 ★★★★★

QLVR was founded specifically to solve the problem of women's performance shoes being resized men's products, and the ENDVR reflects that founding intent in its construction rather than just its marketing copy. The last was developed from female foot data — narrower heel-to-forefoot ratio, wider toe box geometry — not adapted from a unisex template. The WingFit™ system is patented, which at minimum confirms it has undergone a formal development and registration process rather than being a brand name applied to standard elastic.

The knitted eucalyptus-based upper moves with foot swelling patterns that women experience during longer sessions, and the overall construction prioritises fit security over decorative detailing. There are no gratuitous colourway flourishes substituting for engineering here. The brand is women-only by design, not by afterthought. I'd still like to see QLVR publish its last development methodology and confirm whether female athletes or podiatrists were formally consulted — that transparency would lock in this score with zero reservation.

---

### Transparency & User Reviews — 5/5 ★★★★★

QLVR publishes a detailed foot-measurement sizing chart, discloses material composition (bio-based Pebax®, eucalyptus-fibre knit upper), names its patented technology (WingFit™), and is transparent about the sizing adjustment most customers require — a level of honesty that many established brands avoid. The founder, Nicolle Dean, is publicly named, which adds a layer of brand accountability that matters to me. The sustainability claims reference specific, verifiable materials (castor-bean-derived Pebax®, eucalyptus fibres) rather than vague "eco-friendly" language, which is a meaningful step above the greenwashing I encounter regularly. I'd encourage QLVR to publish independent laboratory performance data — drop measurement, stack height, wear-test results — and pursue third-party sustainability certifications (bluesign®, Global Recycled Standard) to fully substantiate their claims at this price point. No independent third-party testing data was available to me at time of writing; I've flagged that in my performance scoring accordingly.

## Who It's For / Who It's Not For

**Who it's for:** Women who've consistently struggled with heel slip, too-narrow toe boxes, or the mid-session lace re-tie — particularly those doing mixed training (running plus gym work) who want one shoe that handles both. If you're drawn to purposeful, considered design and are prepared to size up half a size, this is worth every penny.

**Who it's not for:** Runners who require specified heel-to-toe drop for injury management or gait correction — the absence of that published data is a real barrier. Also not suitable for those who need a wide-fit option (this is a regular-width shoe with a generously shaped toe box, not a true wide fit), or women above a UK 9.

## Size & Fit

I tested in my usual size and found the fit marginally snug across the forefoot on first wear — consistent with QLVR's own guidance. I'd recommend going up half a size as a starting point. If you're between sizes or have a wider foot, go up a full size. Use the foot-measurement chart at qlvr.com rather than relying on your usual trainer size, as sizing does vary between brands. The toe box is notably roomier than most women's trainers I've worn, owing to the female-specific forefoot geometry, but the overall fitting is regular width rather than wide.
