### Open water swimming goggles are one of those pieces of kit where expectations are simple clear vision, a secure fit, and zero distractions once you're in the water. And with so many brands claiming to deliver exactly that, it takes something genuinely different to stand out.

The <a href="https://www.zoggs.com/en_GB/product/predator-flex-polarized-ultra-goggles-461046#u463" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored">Zoggs Predator Flex **Polarised** goggles</a> certainly arrive with that promise, combining comfort focused design with glare reducing lenses aimed at open water swimmers. But while they tick plenty of boxes on paper, the real question is whether they deliver consistently when it matters.

In this review, we take a closer look at where they perform well and where they start to show their limitations.

### Comfort & Fit

These goggles just didn't work for me. Adjustment mid-swim was a nightmare and I found myself stopping in the middle of sessions, fiddling with the strap length and repositioning them on my head to find the one magical spot where they wouldn't leak. Even when they stayed in place, my vision was blurred in the middle, which made for a disorientating swim experience. I suspect this is due to the curved lens feature, which is supposed to expand your field of vision and great in theory, less great when it means you're basically swimming through a foggy bubble.

The colour options are, frankly, uninspiring. I like to coordinate my goggles with my swim hat and cossie so everyone knows exactly which swimmer I am in the pack, but these monotone shades don't give you that fun personal flair.

### Sizing

Unlike other goggle brands, the Zoggs Predator Flex range comes in two sizes, which is helpful if you know which one fits you. Zoggs provide a handy sizing guide here: <a href="https://www.zoggs.com/en_GB/predator-goggle-range" target="_blank" rel="nofollow sponsored">https://www.zoggs.com/en_GB/predator-goggle-range</a>.

I used the regular fit, and it was a mixed bag. Some laps felt fine; others ended with what can only be described as a miniature Niagara Falls pouring into my eyes. The inconsistency made each swim a gamble.

### Functionality

On the upside, as an outdoor swimmer, I appreciated the polarised lenses. They do a good job of filtering the low morning sun without making your view too dark or yellowed. The tint is subtle and pleasant, and when they're not leaking, the goggles are genuinely comfortable on the eyes.

### Value

At £42, these goggles are decent value if you manage to get the right fit. But at that price, you really don't want to make a mistake and unfortunately, getting the right fit isn't straightforward.

### Would I recommend?

If you can test the sizing first, then yes, these goggles could work. Otherwise, proceed with caution.

### Final Verdict

The Zoggs Predator Flex with polarised lenses feels like it was designed with men in mind. The sizing language ("regular" versus "smaller") suggests that women are just scaled-down versions of men, which feels dismissive. If you enjoy leaking goggles and mid-swim adjustments, congratulations, you've found your match.

The webpage doesn't make it easy to link to the sizing guide from the product page, which adds another layer of inconvenience. Personally, I can't wait to give these away to a friend who actually fits them.
