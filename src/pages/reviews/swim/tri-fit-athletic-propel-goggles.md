## WSS™ Assessment

**Pillar 1 — Inclusive Fit & Proportional Sizing: 4/5**

The three-size adjustable TPEE nose bridge is the standout fit feature here, and it matters more than most brands acknowledge. Women's inter-ocular distances typically run shorter than male-default goggle frames assume, so the ability to select a narrower bridge is functionally meaningful rather than cosmetic. The precision-moulded racing gasket created a reliable seal on my face without excessive orbital pressure, and I had no post-swim bruising — a real-world test that larger, stiffer frames often fail. The lightweight TPE frame distributes load well. The score stops at 4 rather than 5 because no frame dimensions, inter-ocular distance data, or lens cup diameter are published anywhere on the product page. Without those numbers, female swimmers with very narrow or wide facial profiles are purchasing blind. That is a data gap the brand should close.

**Pillar 2 — Functional Performance in Use: 4/5**

The ISO 18527-3:2020 anti-fog compliance citation is a concrete, auditable standard — the correct ISO for eye protector anti-fog performance — and in my sessions the lenses did not fog across multiple pool swims. The optical-grade polycarbonate construction delivered genuinely sharp underwater clarity; the REVO blue mirrored coating performed well in outdoor open-water light conditions. The racing gasket held its seal through tumble turns and faster-effort sets without requiring mid-session adjustment. The strap system is simple and quick to lock. I am flagging two unverified claims: "HD clarity" carries no agreed measurable standard, and "fingerprint-free" coating durability is asserted without cited data. The goggles perform well in practice, but those two claims remain marketing language until the brand publishes supporting test data or a defined coating standard.

**Pillar 3 — Adequate Support Standards: 5/5**

For swim goggles, Pillar 3 assesses eye protection standards rather than breast support. The ISO 18527-3:2020 anti-fog compliance is the relevant auditable standard here, and it is explicitly cited. UVA/UVB protection via polycarbonate lenses is consistent with the material class, and the impact-proof and scratch-resistant descriptors are standard and appropriate for PC lens specification. The REVO mirrored coating adds a further UV-reflective layer. No Pillar 3 hard-rule cap is triggered; the goggles meet the protection standard cited.

**Pillar 5 — Genuine Female Design: 3/5**

This is where I have to be direct. There is no published evidence that the PROPEL has been engineered specifically around female facial geometry. The three-size nose bridge is present and useful, but it is also standard specification on many unisex competition goggles. The product listing does not state whether this is a women's-specific design or a unisex performance goggle. No female-specific frame width, cup profile, or fit testing data is referenced. The 220 Triathlon awards do not specify female-specific evaluation criteria. The goggles perform well on a female face — I can confirm that from personal use — but "works on my face" is not the same as "engineered for female facial anatomy." There is no evidence of pink-it-and-shrink-it marketing, which is a genuine positive, but the absence of women-specific design documentation keeps this at Acceptable.

**Pillar 7 — Transparency & User Reviews: 5/5**

The ISO 18527-3:2020 citation is the key differentiator here. Most goggle brands state "anti-fog technology" with no supporting standard; TRI-FIT have cited the specific, correct, verifiable ISO reference. Combined with two independent editorial awards from 220 Triathlon — a publication with a known pool-testing methodology — there is more third-party validation attached to this product than is typical at this price point. The 220 Triathlon editorial verdict ("quality, classy goggles at a good price") is a named, attributed professional quote, not anonymous user content. I am noting that no independent consumer review volume data was available for this pre-assessment, and UV transmission percentage is not published. Those are gaps. However, the ISO citation and dual independent editorial awards justify the top score in this pillar.

## Size & Fit

The PROPEL comes in a single frame size with the primary fit customisation delivered via the three-size TPEE nose bridge. In my testing, the racing gasket created a clean seal without requiring excessive strap tension, and the low-profile TPE frame sat within the orbital area without pressing on the cheekbone. No goggle marks remained after a standard training session.

However, I cannot state with confidence how this frame will perform on narrower or wider female facial profiles because TRI-FIT has not published frame width, cup diameter, or inter-ocular distance specifications. If you have previously found single-size goggles either too wide or too narrow at the temples, I would recommend trying these in person before committing, or confirming the return policy with the retailer prior to purchase.
