## Overview
### A £40 performance goggle that fits women without the male-default compromise

If you've struggled to find a credible mid-price performance goggle that actually fits a female face, the **TRI-FIT Athletic PROPEL** is worth your attention. Male-default frames so often sit too wide and seal poorly across the bridge, leaving you fiddling mid-set. I tested the PROPEL across pool and open-water sessions, looking at seal integrity, anti-fog reliability and whether the three-size nose bridge genuinely solves the fit problem. My overall impression: a properly capable goggle that punches well above its **£40** price, held back only by a few unpublished specs.

## WSS™ Assessment

### Female-Specific Design & Sizing — 3/5 ★★★☆☆

The standout here is the **three-size TPEE nose bridge**, and it matters more than most brands acknowledge. Women's inter-ocular distances typically run shorter than male-default frames assume, so being able to fit a narrower bridge is functionally meaningful rather than cosmetic — on my face the seal sat cleanly with no orbital pressure or post-swim marks. What keeps this at *Acceptable* is that TRI-FIT doesn't publish evidence of female-specific frame engineering, and the adjustable bridge is also standard on many unisex competition goggles. There's no "pink-it-and-shrink-it" gimmickry, which is a genuine plus — but "fits women well" isn't the same as "engineered for female anatomy".

### Functional Performance in Use — 4/5 ★★★★☆

This is where the PROPEL earns its keep. Across multiple pool swims the **optical-grade polycarbonate lenses** stayed sharp and undistorted, and the anti-fog held without that mid-session misting that ruins a set. The **racing gasket** kept its seal through tumble turns and faster-effort work, and the **REVO blue mirrored coating** handled bright outdoor light nicely in open water. The strap locks quickly and stays put. I'd flag that "HD clarity" has no agreed measurable standard and the "fingerprint-free" coating durability isn't backed by cited data — *not independently verified, based on product page claims only* — but in real use these performed reliably.

### Fit, Adjustability & Size Range — 4/5 ★★★★☆

Fit customisation is delivered almost entirely through that three-bridge system, and it works well in practice: I got a secure, comfortable seal without cranking the strap. The low-profile **TPE frame** distributes load evenly and sits within the orbital area rather than digging into the cheekbone. The catch is the single frame size with no published frame width, cup diameter or inter-ocular figures, so swimmers with very narrow or wide profiles are buying a little blind. If you've previously found single-size goggles awkward at the temples, I'd try these on or confirm the return policy first.

### Safety & Performance Standards — 5/5 ★★★★★

This is the PROPEL's strongest card. The **ISO 18527-3:2020** anti-fog compliance is a concrete, auditable standard — the correct ISO for eye-protector anti-fog performance — and it's explicitly cited, where most rivals offer only a vague "anti-fog technology" claim. The polycarbonate lenses carry UVA/UVB protection consistent with the material class, and the impact-resistant, scratch-resistant descriptors are appropriate for a PC lens, with the REVO mirror adding a further UV-reflective layer. Solid, verifiable protection credentials.

### Transparency & User Reviews — 5/5 ★★★★★

The ISO citation is a real differentiator: a specific, correct, verifiable standard rather than marketing shorthand. On top of that sit dual **220 Triathlon 2025** accolades — *Best on Test* and *Gear of the Year* — from a publication with a known pool-testing methodology, plus a named, attributed editorial verdict ("quality, classy goggles at a good price"). That's more third-party validation than is typical at this price. I'll note that no consumer review volume was available for this assessment and the UV transmission percentage isn't published — gaps worth closing — but the verifiable certification data clears any transparency cap.

## Fit & Sizing

The PROPEL comes in a single frame size, with the main fit customisation delivered through the **three-size TPEE nose bridge**. In my testing the racing gasket created a clean seal without excessive strap tension, and the low-profile TPE frame sat comfortably within the orbital area without pressing on the cheekbone — no goggle marks after a standard training session.

That said, I can't tell you with confidence how the frame behaves on much narrower or wider faces, because TRI-FIT hasn't published frame width, cup diameter or inter-ocular distance figures. If single-size goggles have ever felt too wide or too narrow at your temples, try these in person or confirm the retailer's return policy before committing.

## FAQ

### Are the TRI-FIT PROPEL Goggles suitable for open-water swimming?
Yes. The REVO blue mirrored coating managed light reflectance well outdoors, and the racing gasket held its seal in my open-water sessions. They're primarily a pool-performance goggle but cope well in open water too.

### Do the TRI-FIT PROPEL Goggles genuinely not fog?
The anti-fog is backed by ISO 18527-3:2020 — the correct auditable standard for eye-protector anti-fog testing. In my pool sessions they didn't fog. Long-term coating durability across months of training isn't independently verified.

### What nose bridge sizes are included with the TRI-FIT PROPEL?
Three TPEE nose bridges come in the box. This tackles the most common female goggle fit failure point — a shorter inter-ocular distance — though TRI-FIT doesn't publish specific millimetre measurements.

### Are the TRI-FIT PROPEL Goggles a women's-specific design?
Not explicitly confirmed by TRI-FIT's published information. The three-size nose bridge clearly benefits female swimmers, but there's no stated female-specific frame engineering — so I'd call it a performance goggle that fits women well.

### How do the TRI-FIT PROPEL Goggles compare on price?
At £40 they undercut direct rivals by roughly £15–£25 — the Speedo Fastskin Pure Focus sits around £65 and the Arena Cobra Ultra Swipe around £55. That makes the PROPEL strong value for a goggle with a cited anti-fog standard.
