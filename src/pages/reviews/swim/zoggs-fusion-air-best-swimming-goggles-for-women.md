## FAQ

### Are the Zoggs Fusion Air Titanium good goggles for women?
In my testing, yes. The air-cushioned seals and three interchangeable nose bridges let you personalise the fit, which is what makes them so comfortable on narrower or finer female facial features — they're my pick for women who struggle to find a reliable, leak-free seal.

### Do the Zoggs Fusion Air goggles leak?
Across multiple pool and open-water sessions I had zero leaks. The air-filled silicone gasket creates a soft, even seal, and swapping to the right nose bridge size for your face is the key to keeping it leak-free without over-tightening.

### What nose bridge sizes come with the Fusion Air goggles?
Three interchangeable nose bridges are included — small, medium and large. For my narrower face the small bridge was ideal; wider facial spacing suits the medium or large. This is the main reason the fit works so well across different face shapes.

### Are the titanium mirrored lenses worth it for indoor swimming?
The mirrored lenses cut glare brilliantly in bright pools and outdoor open water. If you swim exclusively indoors in low light, you might prefer the non-mirrored version — but for most women swimming in mixed conditions, the mirror is a genuine benefit.

### Is the anti-fog coating on the Zoggs Fusion Air reliable?
It held up well with no fogging issues across my sessions. As with any goggle, the coating lasts longer if you rinse them carefully after each swim and avoid wiping the inside of the lens, which can strip the anti-fog layer.
