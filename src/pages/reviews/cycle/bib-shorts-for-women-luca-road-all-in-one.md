## LUCA Road All-In-One Review

### The women-specific bib short that finally fits the chest

After six years of cycling I'd more or less accepted a quiet trade-off: bib shorts either fit my legs or sat right across my chest, never both. Most women's options felt like men's kit scaled down, with straps that pulled awkwardly across the breasts. The **LUCA Road All-In-One** is the first pair that solved that for me. It's a true women-specific cut — sleek, secure and flattering — with a shaped bib that sits where it should and a pad built for the female form. At £175 it's premium, but it rides like performance equipment, not just apparel.

## WSS™ Assessment

### Inclusive Fit & Proportional Sizing — 3/5 ★★★☆☆

The cut itself is a triumph of *proportional* design: shaped for women's bodies rather than borrowed from a unisex block. I trialled an **XS** as a UK size 8 with a smaller chest and the fit was perfect — and the generous stretch means it would comfortably accommodate larger builds, particularly through the chest. The one thing I can't yet confirm is how far the size range extends at the top and bottom end, so I've scored this on the excellent fit I experienced rather than a verified UK 6–24+ span.

### Functional Performance in Use — 4/5 ★★★★☆

In use, these simply *stay put*. The bibs hold their position beautifully with no creeping or readjusting mid-ride, and the reflective detailing on the straps is a thoughtful safety touch. I rode them both outdoors and on the turbo, and they performed brilliantly across both — lightweight yet structured, with no awkward seam pressure over sustained efforts. They behave exactly as good road kit should.

### Adequate Support Standards — 4/5 ★★★★☆

This is where the women-specific thinking really shows. The bib section is shaped and positioned around the chest so the straps sit comfortably rather than dragging across the breasts — the single biggest comfort win for me. There's enough room to wear a sports bra underneath if you want extra support, though I didn't find it necessary. The hold is secure and stable without ever feeling restrictive.

### Thermal Regulation & Material Performance — 3/5 ★★★☆☆

The **59% polyamide / 41% elastane** fabric strikes a lovely balance of stretch, breathability and durability, and it felt cool and supportive across different training environments. It's lightweight but never flimsy. I'd note that breathability and long-term durability claims aren't independently lab-tested or verified across a set wash count, so I've scored on real ride feel rather than published material data.

### Genuine Female Design — 5/5 ★★★★★

Every detail here is engineered *for women*, not pinked-and-shrunk. The standout is the pad: specifically designed for the female form, larger and more supportive than a men's chamois, with a thoughtful **74% recycled polyester, 13% bamboo charcoal, 13% elastane** composition that balances sustainability with softness. Combined with the chest-aware bib and shaped fit, this is the most genuinely female-first cycling design I've worn.

### Transparency & User Reviews — 4/5 ★★★★☆

LUCA is refreshingly open about what goes into the product: both the fabric and the pad compositions are clearly stated, which makes it easy to know what you're buying. My scoring here rests on documented hands-on WSS testing across road and turbo sessions. Wider independent published performance data isn't available yet, but the disclosed specs and real-world use give plenty to stand behind.
