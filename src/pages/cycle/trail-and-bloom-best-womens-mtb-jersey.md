## Trail Bloom Mesh Back Women's MTB Jersey Review

### A Breathable, Female-First MTB Jersey for Real Trail Days

Finding a mountain bike jersey that's genuinely cut for a woman — not a shrunk-down men's shape in a pastel colour — is harder than it should be, and most of us have a drawer full of jerseys that cling, ride up or run hot on a climb. The [**Trail Bloom**](https://womenssportsstore.com/trail-bloom-mountain-biking-gear-for-women/) **Mesh Back Women's MTB Jersey** takes the opposite route: soft, breathable fabric, a back mesh panel and a properly considered female fit. After long days on the bike, grinding up climbs and stopping for cake, my thesis is simple — this is a comfortable, dependable all-rounder that gets the basics genuinely right.

## WSS™ Assessment

### Inclusive Fit & Proportional Sizing — 4/5 ★★★★☆
This is a real strength. The jersey spans **inclusive UK sizes 8–24**, which is a far wider curve than most women's MTB kit offers, and it's designed with women's proportions in mind rather than scaled down from a unisex block. I found the fit true to size and flattering across a range of shapes, with room to move without looking baggy. A touch more range at either end would earn full marks, but this is already an inclusive, confidence-boosting fit.

### Functional Performance in Use — 4/5 ★★★★☆
On the bike it simply works. The cut is *slightly longer at the back* for coverage when you're leaned over the bars, the mid-length sleeves give good cover without restricting movement, and nothing rode up or shifted across longer rides. The fabric has just enough stretch to move freely whether you're climbing or descending. It feels considered and capable rather than fussy.

### Adequate Support Standards — 3/5 ★★★☆☆
This criterion is built around bust support and impact levels, which simply isn't the job of a trail jersey — so I've scored it neutrally rather than marking it down. You'll pair this with your own sports bra for support, and the jersey layers comfortably over a base layer when you want it. Treat this score as "not applicable" rather than a shortfall.

### Thermal Regulation & Material Performance — 4/5 ★★★★☆
The **mesh back panel** and quick-drying fabric are the standout here, keeping me cool on climbs and warmer days without feeling flimsy or see-through. It handles British riding conditions well: sweaty ascents, cool descents and everything in between. It washes well and held its shape in my use — I've held back from top marks only because the long-term moisture-wicking and durability claims aren't independently lab-verified.

### Genuine Female Design — 4/5 ★★★★☆
This is design *by* riders rather than "pink it and shrink it". The longer back, the flattering-but-not-clingy shape and the breathable panel all feel like decisions made by people who understand what women want from MTB kit. It reads as engineering, not just colour. I'd love to see confirmed functional pockets to round it out, but the female-first thinking is clear and welcome.

### Transparency & User Reviews — 3/5 ★★★☆☆
My conclusions here are grounded in documented WSS hands-on use across real rides, which is what this score rests on. Trail Bloom is open about the inclusive sizing and women-specific intent, and the practical claims held up in wear. Fully independent, published lab data isn't available, so I've kept this at a fair, middle mark and noted those specifics as *not independently verified* — not as a failing, simply as missing.
