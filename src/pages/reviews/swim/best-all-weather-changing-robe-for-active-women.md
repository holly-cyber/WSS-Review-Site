## KitBrix All Weather Warming Changing Robe Review

### A Cosy, Zip-Free Recycled Robe for Cold-Water Swimmers

Getting changed after an outdoor swim can be a whole event in itself: cold fingers, wind targeting every inch of skin, and that awkward towel shuffle we've all perfected. The **KitBrix All Weather Warming Changing Robe** solves exactly that for active women who want to step out of the water and into warmth without a fight. It's become my go-to for open-water swims and post-session lounging. My overall take? A cosy, cleverly designed and genuinely sustainable robe that makes cold-water changing feel like a small luxury rather than a chore.

## WSS™ Assessment

### Inclusive Fit & Proportional Sizing — 3/5 ★★★☆☆

The cut is *generous but not shapeless*, which is exactly what you want from a changing robe. At 170 cm and a UK 14 I wear a **Medium** and it hits mid-calf with room to layer underneath without feeling swamped. The fit struck a lovely balance for me, though I'd love to see the full size span clearly confirmed across the range, so I've scored this neutrally as a solid, roomy fit rather than a fully verified inclusive one.

### Functional Performance in Use — 4/5 ★★★★☆

This is where the robe shines. The **poncho style** skips the zip entirely, so you can throw it on with numb hands and no fiddling: a small thing that makes a big difference at the water's edge. There's a large front pocket for warming your hands and a hidden zipped pocket for keys or phone, plus roomy sleeves that make changing underneath easy. It just *works*, without fuss.

### Adequate Support Standards — 3/5 ★★★☆☆

Impact support isn't really the job of a changing robe, so this criterion doesn't apply in the usual sense and I've scored it neutrally. What I will say is the robe does its own kind of supporting work: shelter from the wind, warmth on tap, and enough coverage to change modestly back at the car.

### Thermal Regulation & Material Performance — 4/5 ★★★★☆

The soft green fleece lining is *snuggly without being heavy*, while the breathable outer shell keeps wind and drizzle at bay. Both the shell and lining are made from recycled materials, and the inner fleece is quick-drying and moisture-wicking, so even thrown on straight after a swim it doesn't stay damp for long. After several uses it still feels fresh and dries faster than most robes I've owned, though wash-cycle durability isn't independently lab-verified.

### Genuine Female Design — 4/5 ★★★★☆

This feels like a robe designed *with* women in mind, not just shrunk and recoloured. The cut is flattering rather than "walking duvet", the branding is refreshingly minimal (just a discreet emblem on the back, no loud logos), and the **deep-cut hood** is big enough to fit over a chunky hat or bun while keeping the wind off your neck. Thoughtful, functional touches throughout.

### Transparency & User Reviews — 3/5 ★★★☆☆

The eco credentials are clearly stated, with the outer shell made from **100% recycled plastic bottles**, and my hands-on use backs up the warmth and quick-drying claims. Independent published testing data isn't available to confirm every performance figure, so I've noted that neutrally rather than as a failing. My score here rests on real, sustained use rather than lab numbers.

## Who It's For / Who It's Not For

It's for outdoor women who change at the water's edge: open-water swimmers, paddleboarders, dog-walkers, triathletes and frosty-morning spectators who want warmth and zero fuss. If you specifically want a *zipped* robe, a fully waterproof shell, or a confirmed extended size range before buying, you may want to weigh those points first.

## Size & Fit

I'm 170 cm tall and a UK size 14, and a **Medium** is spot on for me: mid-calf coverage, roomy enough to layer, but never swamping. The deep hood comfortably fits over a hat or bun, and the overall silhouette stays clean and discreet.

## FAQ

### Does the KitBrix changing robe run true to size?

For me, yes. At 170 cm and a UK 14 I wear a Medium and it hits mid-calf with room to layer. If you want extra space to change underneath, size up for a roomier feel.

### Is the KitBrix robe a poncho or a zip-up?

It's a poncho style with no zip. You slip it over your head, which is far easier with cold, numb hands and removes the zip as the part that usually fails first.

### Is the changing robe waterproof?

The outer shell is tough and water-resistant, so it shrugs off wind and light rain well. It's a warm, protective robe rather than a fully waterproof storm shell.

### What is the KitBrix robe made from?

The outer shell is made from 100% recycled plastic bottles and the fleece lining is also recycled, yet it still feels premium, warm and quick-drying.

### Who is the KitBrix changing robe best for?

Open-water swimmers, paddleboarders, triathletes and frosty-morning spectators who want a warm, low-fuss robe. Less ideal if you specifically want a zipped, fully waterproof robe.
