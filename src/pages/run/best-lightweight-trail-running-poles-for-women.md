## Harrier Helvellyn PRO Carbon Z-Poles V2 Review

### Featherweight Carbon Trail Running Poles for Long Climbs

Trail running poles can be either game-changing or that awkward bit of kit you regret halfway up the first climb. As someone who's hauled both brilliant and bargain-bin poles up mountains, I know which side I want to be on. The **Harrier Helvellyn PRO Carbon Z-Poles V2** land firmly in the "why didn't I get these sooner?" camp: ultra-light, packable and genuinely supportive when the gradient bites. For women tackling ultras and big adventure days, they take the strain off your legs without draining your bank account — and that combination is rarer than it should be.

## WSS™ Assessment

### Female-Specific Design & Sizing — 2/5 ★★☆☆☆
Let me be clear about what these are: a length-based pole offered in **six fixed-length options**, not a female-specific design proportioned for a woman's frame. There's no women's-particular geometry here, so this scores neutrally rather than as a failing — you simply pick the right length for your height. What I do credit is that they're built by **Harrier**, a British brand founded by Kate Parker that visibly listens to trail runners, women included. The sizing choice is sensible and the brand intent is welcome, but the poles themselves are essentially unisex.

### Functional Performance in Use — 4/5 ★★★★☆
This is where they shine. From **138g per pole**, they're remarkably light, yet they feel reassuringly sturdy when you lean in hard on a steep, lung-busting climb. The **Z-fold design** snaps open with a slick "click" and the carbon shafts give a reliable boost on every ascent and a confidence-inspiring brace on technical descents. Built for **ultras, mountain marathons and big adventure days**, they do exactly the job you want from a long-day pole.

### Fit, Adjustability & Size Range — 3/5 ★★★☆☆
The **six fixed lengths** give a good spread of sizing, so most women should find a length that suits. The trade-off is that fixed-length carbon doesn't adjust on the fly — carbon poles don't bend, so precision matters, and Harrier's size guide is worth checking carefully before you buy. Once you've got the right length the fit is spot-on, but there's no telescoping wiggle room if you share poles or want to tune the height mid-run.

### Safety & Performance Standards — 3/5 ★★★☆☆
The carbon construction feels premium and held up confidently under load when I leaned my weight into the climbs. The **integrated mud baskets clip together neatly**, and the Z-fold mechanism locks cleanly. I'd note that specific stress-testing data and formal certification figures aren't published — *not independently verified, based on product page and hands-on use only* — so treat the carbon with the care any lightweight pole deserves and you should be fine.

### Transparency & User Reviews — 3/5 ★★★☆☆
Harrier is refreshingly open about who they are and who they build for, and the wider picture is encouraging: as other reviewers have repeatedly mentioned, you'd need to spend double to match this spec from big-name brands. That chimes with my own hands-on impressions. Formal published test methodology is light, but the runner feedback and real-world use back up the performance claims well.

## Who It's For / Who It's Not For

These are for women stepping into poles for the first time or upgrading from heavier alloy: ultra runners, mountain-marathon hopefuls and big-day adventurers who want featherweight support that packs away small. They're less ideal if you need adjustable-length poles to share between people, or if you're hard on your kit and would rather have the forgiveness of alloy over the stiffness of carbon.

## Fit & Sizing

These poles come in **six fixed-length options**, so you choose your length for a precise, no-compromise fit — just check Harrier's size guide carefully first, because carbon doesn't bend and there's no adjustment afterwards. When folded they pack down to a tidy **38cm**, small enough to clip to your vest or slip into hand luggage. If you've ever cursed at tangled poles mid-race, you'll appreciate that the **integrated mud baskets clip together neatly** to keep everything in order.

## FAQ

### How light are the Harrier Helvellyn PRO Carbon Z-Poles V2?
They start from 138g per pole, which puts them firmly in featherweight carbon territory. You barely notice the weight on your vest, yet they still feel sturdy when you lean in on steep climbs.

### Are these poles women-specific?
Not exactly. They come in six fixed lengths rather than a female-specific design, so you choose the right size for your height. They're made by Harrier, a British brand founded by Kate Parker that listens to trail runners, women included.

### How small do they pack down?
Very small. The Z-fold design collapses them to roughly the size of a travel coffee cup, folding to around 38cm, so they clip to a race vest or slip into hand luggage without fuss.

### What are the Harrier poles best for?
Ultras, mountain marathons and big adventure days. They give a reliable boost on long ascents and technical descents, taking strain off your legs over distance, while staying light enough to stash when you don't need them.

### Are carbon poles durable?
Carbon is light and stiff but never indestructible. Treat these with care and they should last through countless races and hikes; just avoid crushing or levering them, as carbon doesn't bend the way alloy does.
