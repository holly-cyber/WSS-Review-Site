## Panterre OP-16 Organic Protein Review

### A Genuinely Clean, UK-Made Organic Protein for Active Women

Finding a protein I actually trust has been harder than it should be. Since my breast cancer diagnosis I've become especially mindful of what I put in my body, and so much of what's on the shelf is loaded with artificial sweeteners, colourings and fillers I'd rather avoid. **Panterre OP-16 Organic Protein** is different: all-natural, organic, UK-manufactured and refreshingly free of the usual additives. After living with it as part of my daily routine, it's become a powder I reach for with confidence — clean, filling and kind to my stomach.

The *clean ingredient list* is the headline for me: organic, made in the UK for proper traceability, and free of artificial colourings and additives. For someone determined to keep their diet natural, that purity matters enormously.

### Performance — 4/5 ★★★★☆
In practice, OP-16 does exactly what I want a protein to do. It's pleasingly filling, keeping me satisfied for a sustained period, and — crucially — it never left me bloated or with the stomach upset some supplements cause. I've used it happily both as a post-workout recovery shake and as the base of a breakfast smoothie, and it's performed reliably in both roles.

### Value — 4/5 ★★★★☆
Given the quality of the ingredients and the protein per serving, I think OP-16 offers good value. It may not be the cheapest powder on the shelf, but the organic certification and the absence of unnecessary additives justify the price for anyone prioritising clean nutrition. For me, paying a little more for something I trust is money well spent.

## Who It's For / Who It's Not For
This is for active women who want a *purely natural* protein and care deeply about what's on the label — anyone seeking organic, additive-free nutrition with a strong protein hit. It's ideal if you value simplicity, clean ingredients and a powder that's easy on the gut.

It's less suited to you if you crave bold, dessert-like flavours, or if rock-bottom price is your single deciding factor.

## Taste & Usage
**Panterre OP-16** is at its best in milk or a smoothie. My favourite blend is full-fat milk with bananas and blueberries: smooth, satisfying and pleasantly mild. As a post-workout recovery shake it slots in easily, and it makes a dependable breakfast base too. The cacao and cinnamon notes are gentle rather than punchy, so if you like a sweeter, bolder hit you might add your own fruit or a little extra flavour. One thing I'd avoid: mixing it into Greek yoghurt, where the texture and colour didn't work for me.
